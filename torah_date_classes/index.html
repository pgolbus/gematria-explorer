Words by Day of Creation

All numbers are either odd or even: either it divides neatly by two or it has one left over. You can do the same thing with respect to three: it divides neatly, has one left over, or has two left over. Just like dividing by two split every number into two groups: odd and even, dividing by three splits every number into three groups called equivalence classes: [[0]], [[1]], and [[2]]. In fact, you can do this with any number, which we call ℤ / nℤ, pronounced "z mod n z." Since all Hebrew words have a numerical value, we can also define Hebrew / nHebrew.

There is nothing particularly significant about whether a number is odd or even, or whether it lands in [[0]], [[1]], …, [[n-1]]. This is not, however, necessarily the case for words.

Let n = 7, the number of days in the week. Then the equivalence classes of Hebrew / nHebrew correspond to the days of the week. Using the tool below, you can explore the words in each day of creation, including [[Shabbat]].
